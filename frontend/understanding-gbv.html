Understanding Gender-Based Violence (GBV)

Your guide to recognizing, preventing, and addressing GBV.

What is GBV?

Gender-Based Violence (GBV) refers to harmful acts directed at individuals based on their gender. It is rooted in gender inequality, power imbalances, and societal norms. GBV can take many forms, including physical, sexual, psychological, and economic abuse.

Types of GBV

  • Physical Violence: Hitting, beating, or causing bodily harm.
  • Sexual Violence: Rape, sexual harassment, or coercion.
  • Psychological Abuse: Threats, insults, or emotional manipulation.
  • Economic Abuse: Controlling finances, denying access to resources.
  • Harmful Practices: Forced marriage, female genital mutilation (FGM).

Causes of GBV

GBV stems from societal structures and individual behaviors. Key causes include:

  • Gender inequality and discrimination.
  • Cultural norms that perpetuate violence.
  • Economic dependency and poverty.
  • Lack of education and awareness.

Dangers and Effects of GBV

GBV has severe consequences for individuals and communities, including:

  • Physical injuries and chronic health problems.
  • Emotional trauma and mental health disorders.
  • Economic instability and poverty.
  • Social stigmatization and isolation.

How to Avoid GBV

Preventing GBV requires collective effort. Here are some steps you can take:

  • Educate yourself and others about gender equality.
  • Promote respectful communication in relationships.
  • Advocate for community awareness programs.
  • Support laws and policies that combat GBV.

What to Do in Case of GBV

If you or someone you know experiences GBV:

  • Reach out to a trusted individual or organization.
  • Call emergency helplines for immediate support.
  • Report the incident to authorities or legal aid services.
  • Seek counseling and medical assistance.
